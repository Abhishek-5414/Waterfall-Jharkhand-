PERWAGHAGH FALL

Perwaghagh Falls is a beautiful waterfall located in the state of Jharkhand, India. It is situated in the Khunti district, approximately 70 km from the capital city of Ranchi. Lush green forests surround the waterfall, and it is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.

The Perwaghagh Falls is a multi-tiered waterfall, with water cascading down a series of rocky steps from around 20 meters. The water flows through the rocks and creates a natural pool at the bottom, perfect for swimming and dipping in the cool water.

The surrounding forest is home to various flora and fauna, including several species of birds, butterflies, and monkeys. Visitors can take a leisurely walk through the woods and explore the area's natural beauty.

The waterfall is accessible by a short trek through the forest. The hike could be more strenuous, and the path is well-defined, making it suitable for visitors of all ages. The best time to visit Perwaghagh Falls is during the monsoon season, when the waterfall is at its full flow and the surrounding forests are lush green and vibrant.

Visitors can also enjoy a picnic at the waterfall, as several picnic spots are nearby. Small food stalls also sell local snacks and refreshments, adding to the overall experience.

Perwaghagh Falls is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. It offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and is a great place to relax and rejuvenate amidst the beauty of nature.
>> Height of Waterfall: 20 metre
>> Entry Fee: Free
>> Timing: 6:30 AM-4 PM
>> How to Reach: car or bus
>> Best Time to Visit: monsoon season
>> Things to Do: picnic
>> Ideal trip duration: 2-3 hours

LOWER GHAGHARI WATERFALL

Lower Ghaghri Falls is located 10 kilometres from Netarhat in Latehar district in the Indian state of Jharkhand. It is the 33rd highest waterfall in India. UPPER GHAGHARI WATERFALL

Upper Ghaghri Waterfall is a stunning cascade located near the picturesque hill station of Netarhat in the Latehar district of Jharkhand, India. Often referred to as the 'Queen of Chotanagpur,' Netarhat is renowned for its sublime beauty and serene environment, with the Upper Ghaghri Waterfall being one of its prime attractions. The waterfall emerges from the Ghaghri River and majestically drops from a height of about 98 feet amidst lush greenery. This hidden gem is surrounded by dense forests that are part of the Netarhat Plateau, which is rich in biodiversity and offers a refreshing retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life. The waterfall's clear and cool water creates a tranquil ambiance, perfect for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts. SUKHALDARI FALL

Sukhdari Waterfalls is one of the prime sites of Garhwa District in Jharkhand. It is situated on the Kanhar River, and borders the states of Chhattisgarh and Uttar Pradesh.

Tamasin Waterfall

Tamasin Waterfall is a beautiful waterfall in the state of Jharkhand in eastern India. It is situated in the Ranchi district, around 40 km from the capital city of Ranchi.

The waterfall is a popular tourist attraction with lush green forests and scenic views. The Tamasin River forms the Tamasin waterfall as it cascades down from around 30 meters. The waterfall is best visited during the monsoon season when the water flow peaks and the surrounding forests are at their greenest. Visitors can enjoy the refreshing mist and the sound of the water as it falls over the rocks.

The area around the Tamasin waterfall is home to diverse flora and fauna. The forests around the waterfall teem with wildlife, such as deer, monkeys, and various bird species. Visitors can enjoy a nature walk through the woods and spot some animals in their natural habitat.

>> Height of Waterfall: 150 feet
>> Entry Fee: Free
>> Timing: 6 AM - 6 PM
>> How to Reach: bike and car
>> Best Time to Visit: monsoon or winter season
>> Things to Do: picnic
>> Ideal trip duration: 2-4 hours
